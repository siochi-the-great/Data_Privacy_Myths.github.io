Location Privacy Tips

Concerned about your digital footprint? Here's how to regain control over your location data, reduce exposure, and maintain your privacy online and offline.

1. Disable Location for Unnecessary Apps

Review app permissions and disable location access for apps that don’t need it. According to Mozilla’s Privacy Not Included project, many popular apps collect location data even when not actively used.

Tip: On Android, go to Settings > Location > App location permissions. On iOS, use Settings > Privacy & Security > Location Services.

2. Turn Off Bluetooth and Wi-Fi When Not Needed

Bluetooth beacons and Wi-Fi scans can reveal your location even without a connection. Both Android and iOS allow background scanning unless manually disabled.

3. Use a VPN to Mask Your IP Location

VPNs (Virtual Private Networks) encrypt your traffic and mask your IP address, making it harder for websites to determine your physical location. This is especially useful on public Wi-Fi.

Fact: A 2022 Consumer Reports test showed some free VPNs log location data themselves. Choose a no-log provider like ProtonVPN or Mullvad.

4. Clear Location History Regularly

Google, Apple, and Facebook store your location history. Clear these histories regularly and consider disabling location history entirely in your account settings.

5. Use Privacy-First Alternatives

  • Use DuckDuckGo instead of Google for searches (no location tracking).
  • Use Signal or Threema instead of apps like Messenger.
  • Install privacy dashboards like Exodus Privacy to audit app trackers.

6. Use Airplane Mode in Sensitive Locations

This disables most forms of active tracking (GPS, cell towers, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth). Great when visiting sensitive places such as clinics, legal offices, or activist events.

Pro Insight

In 2023, an academic paper by the University of Toronto found that simply turning off GPS reduced location leakage by only 37%—real protection comes from cutting multiple channels.
