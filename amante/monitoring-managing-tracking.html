Monitoring and Managing Tracking

Understanding how you're tracked online and through your devices is the first step to regaining control of your privacy.

How Are You Being Tracked?

Companies use a combination of device sensors, metadata, and digital identifiers to track users. Common tools include:

  • Advertising IDs – unique identifiers used by apps and advertisers
  • Cookies – browser files storing preferences and activity logs
  • Device Fingerprinting – analyzing unique configurations like fonts, screen resolution, and installed plugins
  • Location Data – collected through GPS, Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and IP address triangulation

Tools to Monitor Tracking

Several apps and browser extensions can help detect and block tracking attempts:

Recommended Tools

  • DuckDuckGo Privacy Browser: Blocks trackers and enforces HTTPS (source: duckduckgo.com)
  • Privacy Badger: Developed by EFF, blocks invisible trackers (source: eff.org)
  • GlassWire: Monitors network activity and app behavior (source: glasswire.com)
  • Jumbo Privacy: Analyzes your social media privacy settings (source: jumboprivacy.com)

Managing Tracking Settings

Even without third-party tools, you can reduce tracking via settings on your devices:

  1. Disable ad personalization: Found in Android and iOS privacy settings
  2. Turn off location services for unused apps
  3. Clear cookies regularly or use incognito browsing
  4. Limit background app activity to reduce passive data collection
  5. Use VPNs to mask your IP address and browsing activity
Note: In 2022, a Mozilla report found that even with location services off, 54% of top Android apps collected approximate location data through other means. (Source: Mozilla Foundation, 2022)

Best Practices Summary

  • Use privacy-first tools and browsers
  • Restrict app permissions
  • Review device and account privacy dashboards monthly
  • Stay informed on how new tech features affect tracking
