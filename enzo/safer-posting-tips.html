Safer Posting Tips Online

Online safety is more important than ever in this modern digital era. It's important to take protects to maintain your data and privacy whether you're sharing information, interacting with people, or browsing the web. These internet safety guidelines were gathered from reliable sources and platforms.

1. Stay Vigilant Against Phishing Scams

Phishing scams are fraudulent attempts to steal private information by impersonating as reliable organizations. When visiting links in emails, messages, or pop-up windows, exercise caution at all times. Make sure the webpage is authentic and confirm the sender.

2. Use Strong and Unique Passwords

It's important to create secure, one-of-a-kind passwords for every account you have. Passwords must be sufficiently complex, contain a variety of characters, and keep clear of information that could be guessed. To securely generate and store passwords, consider implementing a password manager.

3. Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

Two-factor Authentication demands an additional proof of identity, like a code texted to your phone, to better secure your accounts. It restricts hackers' ability to access your data.

4. Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Transactions

Because public Wi-Fi networks are less secure, they can be harmful. Stay clear of using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions like online banking or shopping. If you must use public networks, use a VPN to ensure a secure connection..

5. Be Careful About What You Share Online

Consider the possibilities before publishing geographical data, photos, or personal information online. Cyberbullying and identity theft can result from oversharing. Make sure that whatever you share is appropriate and doesn't violate your privacy.
