What VPNs Can Do:
  • Encrypt your data: Makes it unreadable to outsiders like hackers and ISPs, especially on public Wi-Fi.
  • Mask your IP address: Hides your real location by routing traffic through a VPN server.
  • Bypass some geo-restrictions: Lets you access content blocked in your region, but not always reliably.
  • Prevent ISP throttling: Your ISP can’t see what you’re doing, so it’s harder for them to slow you down.
What VPNs Cannot Do:
  • Make you completely anonymous: Sites can still track you using cookies, fingerprinting, and logins.
  • Protect against malware or phishing: VPNs don’t scan downloads or block harmful links unless bundled with extra tools.
  • Bypass all geo-blocks: Some sites detect and block VPN usage.
  • Stop all tracking: Browser fingerprinting, tracking pixels, and account logins can still follow you.
  • Guarantee no logging: Some VPNs still collect minimal data. Trust only independently audited providers.
  • Improve internet speed always: VPN encryption and server distance can sometimes slow things down.
Common Misconceptions:
  • “VPNs make me 100% safe online” → False. They’re just one layer of protection.
  • “VPNs are illegal” → Mostly false. They’re legal in most places, but illegal activity remains illegal.
  • “All VPNs are the same” → False. They vary in speed, privacy, and reliability.
  • “I don’t need a VPN because I have nothing to hide” → Misguided. VPNs help protect against data theft too.
  • “Free VPNs are fine” → Risky. Many log and sell your data.
  • “VPNs block all data leaks” → Not true. DNS leaks and fingerprinting still happen without extra precautions.
Real Risks VPNs Don’t Eliminate:
  • Data leaks from browser extensions, misconfigured apps, or insecure settings.
  • DNS leaks that expose what websites you visit.
  • Browser fingerprinting that tracks you even without cookies.
  • Tracking from apps and accounts like Google or Facebook.
  • Email tracking pixels that notify senders when you open messages.
What You Should Use Alongside a VPN:
  • Privacy-focused browsers (e.g., Firefox) with extensions like uBlock Origin and Privacy Badger.
  • Strong antivirus and anti-malware protection.
  • Secure, unique passwords with two-factor authentication.
  • Be cautious of sketchy sites, apps, and suspicious links.
  • Regularly run DNS leak and fingerprinting tests.
