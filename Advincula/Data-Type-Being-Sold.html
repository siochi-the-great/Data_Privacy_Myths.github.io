How Is Data Being Sold?

Data brokers are companies that gather and sell detailed personal information about individuals to businesses or organizations. This data can include anything from your name, birthdate, and job title to your interests, shopping habits, medical history, and even online activities like browsing and social media use. They collect this information through public records, partnerships with other companies (such as credit card firms), and by scanning online sources like social media platforms. The data brokerage industry generates over $200 billion in revenue each year and continues to grow as more data is produced daily. Companies use this information to create targeted advertising, such as knowing when you last bought laundry detergent or what games you enjoy, allowing them to send ads at the most strategic time.

What is “user consent” and why is it required?

User consent is the explicit permission individuals give for the collection, use, storage, and possible sharing of their personal data. It’s essential that users are clearly informed about how their data will be handled, including the purpose of collection, how long it will be retained, and how it may benefit them. Gaining consent isn’t just about compliance — it’s also key to building trust. When users understand how their data is used to enhance their experience, such as through personalized offers or product improvements, they are more likely to agree.

In today’s digital landscape, privacy regulations like GDPR, CPRA, and LGPD play a major role in shaping how consent must be obtained and managed. Depending on your role — whether as a data controller or a data processor — your responsibilities will vary. Controllers must establish and communicate clear data policies, while processors must follow those policies and ensure compliance.

With different regions enforcing different rules, maintaining global compliance can be challenging. That’s why using tools and platforms designed with privacy and regulatory compliance in mind is critical to responsibly managing user data, no matter where your users are located.
