Verified facts and notable rumors about Facebook

Verified facts about Facebook:

1. Founding and Evolution
  • Facebook was founded in 2004 by Mark Zuckerberg, Eduardo Saverin, Andrew McCollum, Dustin Moskovitz, and Chris Hughes. Originally launched as "TheFacebook," it rebranded to "Facebook" in August 2005.
    • 2. User Base and Engagement
  • As of 2024, Facebook boasts over 3.07 billion monthly active users (MAUs) and 2.11 billion daily active users (DAUs), making it the world's most-used social platform. The platform maintains 2.11 billion daily active users, accounting for approximately 68.7% of its monthly user base.
  • In Q2 2024, Meta (Facebook's parent company) reported a revenue of $39.07 billion, marking a 22% year-over-year increase. The company's net income for the same period was $13.46 billion, a significant 73% increase from Q1 2023.

    • Rumors and Controversies:

    1. Antitrust Lawsuit and Market Dominance
  • The Business Insider article titled "Meta's Defense in Antitrust Trial Gets Off to a Zesty, Animated Start" provides an overview of the opening arguments in the Federal Trade Commission's (FTC) antitrust lawsuit against Meta Platforms, Inc. The trial, which began on April 14, 2025, centers on the FTC's allegations that Meta's acquisitions of Instagram in 2012 and WhatsApp in 2014 were strategic moves to eliminate competition and maintain a monopoly in the social media market.
    • Trial Proceedings is taking place in a federal court in Washington, D.C., with U.S. District Judge James Boasberg presiding. The outcome could have significant implications for Meta's business operations and the broader tech industry.
