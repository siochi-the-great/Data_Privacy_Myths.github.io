What Is A Data Leak?

A data leak happens when an internal party or source exposes sensitive data, usually unintentionally or by accident. The results of a data leak can range from loss of information to malicious exploitation. Often, data leaks lead to data breaches.

Causes:

  • Poorly configured privacy settings
  • Weak VPNs or proxy tools
  • Malicious browser extensions

Risks:

  • Identity exposure
  • Tracking by advertisers or attackers

What Is DNS?

DNS, standing for "Domain Name System," is a distributed database that provides a translation service between hostnames and IP addresses. On the Internet, every computer has a unique IP address, just like every person has a unique identification number. However, IP addresses are composed of a series of numbers, such as 192.168.1.1, which are not easy for humans to remember.

How It Happens:

  • Misconfigured VPNs
  • Operating system-level DNS settings

Risks:

  • Reveals websites you visit
  • Undermines anonymity
  • Allows censorship or surveillance

What Is Fingerprinting?

Fingerprints are the unique pattern of curves and ridges indented into the skin of a person’s fingertips. Most people have completely unique fingerprints, which can help with identification.

Methods:

  • Canvas fingerprinting
  • AudioContext fingerprinting
  • User agent & plugin tracking

Risks:

  • Persistent tracking even without cookies
  • Difficult to block or reset

How to Protect Yourself

  • Use strong, no-log VPNs
  • Test for DNS leaks regularly
  • Use privacy-focused browsers (e.g., Firefox with add-ons)
  • Enable anti-fingerprinting tools or extensions (e.g., uBlock Origin, Privacy Badger)
  • Avoid unnecessary plugins
