Texting on cell phones has become the preferred way of communication amongst the younger generation. Now with more sophisticated phones and smart devices, texting has spilled over into the business world and become a hit with adults. As new cell phones come out, networks are offering better solutions and packages for this new technical obsession. Some may wonder what the technology behind text messaging is or how it is possible.

Sending a Message

  • The message is turned into digital data.
  • This data is encrypted (if the app supports it).
  • It’s sent through the internet to the app’s server.

Delivery to Receiver

  • The message is decrypted (if encrypted).
  • Delivered to the recipient’s phone using internet or mobile network.
  • Push notifications alert them of the new message.

End-to-End Encryption (E2EE)

  • Only the sender and recipient can read the message.
  • Even the server cannot see it—it only delivers the message.
  • Messages are encrypted before they leave your device.

Cellular Messaging (SMS)

  • The message goes through your mobile carrier’s control towers.
  • No encryption is used by default.
  • Messages are briefly stored on the Short Message Service Center (SMSC).
